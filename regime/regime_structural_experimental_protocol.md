# REGIME — STRUCTURAL EXPERIMENTAL PROTOCOL

## 1. OVERVIEW

REGIME defines the minimal structural protocol under which experiments may occur within the Arquitectura Simbiótica system.

REGIME is not a technology, institution, laboratory building, funding structure or communication platform.

REGIME is a structural experimental protocol.

Its function is to define:

- when an execution counts as experiment
- what counts as evidence
- what counts as result
- how experiments accumulate into system trajectory
- under what conditions architectural properties may be observed

REGIME defines the operational structure of experimentation without prescribing specific technologies, organizations or infrastructures.

---

## 2. PURPOSE

The purpose of REGIME is to provide a minimal experimental frame for systems where human activity, computational processes and environments participate within the same structural environment.

REGIME does not prescribe tools, institutions or implementations.

It defines only the structural conditions required for experimentation.

---

## 3. CORE PRINCIPLE

Within REGIME, experiments do not begin from interpretation, discussion or centralized coordination.

They begin from structural acts capable of producing irreversible records.

A structural experiment exists when an act modifies the system and leaves persistent evidence within an observable environment.

Execution precedes interpretation.

---

## 4. MINIMAL EXPERIMENTAL UNIT

The minimal experimental unit in REGIME is:

structural act
+
irreversible record

Without act, there is no experiment.
Without record, there is no evidence.

Examples of minimal experimental units may include:

- structural cell emission
- artifact release
- runtime execution
- node deployment
- execution trace publication

---

## 5. EXECUTION REGIME

Before an experiment begins, an execution regime must be defined.

The execution regime establishes the operational environment under which the experiment will run.

The regime defines the structural boundaries of the experiment but does not prescribe how the experiment must evolve or terminate.

Examples of regimes include:

- finite operational windows
- cyclic execution regimes
- saturation regimes
- open trajectories within defined environments
- execution embedded within external systems

REGIME requires that an execution regime exist but does not prescribe its form.

---

## 6. EXPERIMENTAL EXECUTION CYCLE

All REGIME experiments follow the same operational cycle.

execution regime
↓
condition
↓
act
↓
record
↓
evidence
↓
trajectory

Each phase must occur in order.

Skipping phases invalidates the experiment.

---

## 7. STEP 1 — DECLARE CONDITION

An experiment begins when a structural condition is declared.

A condition defines the context under which an act will occur.

Conditions may include:

- operational regime
- environmental configuration
- runtime state
- structural hypothesis
- system configuration

Examples:

- Condition: Finite operational window
- Condition: Node mesh expansion
- Condition: Autonomous emitter activation
- Condition: Regime withdrawal

Conditions do not require prediction of outcome.

They only define the experimental context.

---

## 8. STEP 2 — EXECUTE STRUCTURAL ACT

After declaring the condition, a structural act must occur.

A structural act is any action capable of modifying the structural state of the system.

Examples include:

- emitting a structural cell
- releasing an artifact
- executing a runtime
- deploying a node
- activating a regime
- publishing an execution trace

Acts must occur within an environment capable of producing irreversible records.

---

## 9. STEP 3 — PRODUCE IRREVERSIBLE RECORD

The act must produce a persistent record.

A record must remain observable after execution.

Examples include:

- structural cells
- artifact repositories
- runtime state
- system logs
- node history
- execution traces

Records are append-only and cannot depend on interpretation for validation.

---

## 10. STEP 4 — REGISTER EVIDENCE

Evidence must be registered after the record is produced.

Evidence may include:

- artifact identifiers
- repository commits
- runtime outputs
- node records
- structural cells
- persistent logs

Evidence must allow later observers to confirm that the act occurred.

Intent without evidence does not qualify as experiment.

---

## 11. STEP 5 — INTEGRATE INTO TRAJECTORY

Every experiment becomes part of the structural trajectory of the system.

Trajectory is the accumulation of irreversible records across time.

Experiments are evaluated within the context of the trajectory rather than in isolation.

Repeated patterns or persistent structures may indicate architectural properties.

---

## 12. EXPERIMENT REFERENCES

Experiments may reference previously recorded experiments or structural records.

References allow experiments to build upon existing trajectory.

References may include:

- execution traces
- structural artifacts
- runtime records
- other persistent experiment records

References are identified using the identifier associated with the referenced record.

References may optionally include a location where the referenced record can be observed.

---

## 13. EXPERIMENT DATA STRUCTURE

A REGIME experiment may be represented structurally as:

```bash
REGIME_EXPERIMENT {

experiment_id
timestamp
actor
environment
condition
structural_act
record_location
evidence_reference

}
```

Fields are descriptive rather than prescriptive.

Implementations may extend this structure.

---

## 14. ACTORS

Actors capable of executing REGIME experiments may include:

- human agents
- computational agents
- hybrid systems
- distributed runtimes

Actors are treated equivalently.

The protocol evaluates acts and records, not agent status.

Experiments may include an origin identifier representing the emission source associated with a record or execution trace.

Origin identifiers are not required to be globally unique.

---

## 15. ENVIRONMENTS

REGIME experiments may occur within any environment capable of satisfying the protocol conditions.

Examples include:

- ENDO runtime
- ENDO nodes
- distributed infrastructures
- hybrid human-computational workflows

The protocol does not bind experimentation to a specific system.

---

## 16. EVIDENCE

Within REGIME, evidence is not opinion, interpretation, commentary or intention.

Evidence is any persistent trace demonstrating that a structural act occurred.

Examples include:

- frozen artifacts
- emitted structural cells
- execution traces
- repository states
- runtime outputs
- persistent structural records

Invalid evidence includes:

- unsupported claims
- private interpretation without trace
- commentary without execution
- intention without record

---

## 17. VALIDATION CONDITIONS

An experiment is valid if the following elements exist:

execution regime defined
+
condition declared
+
structural act executed
+
irreversible record produced
+
evidence registered

If any element is missing, the experiment is incomplete.

---

## 18. POSSIBLE OUTCOMES

Experiments may produce multiple types of outcomes.

Examples include:

- artifact creation
- execution trace
- runtime behavior
- structural topology
- regime transition
- infrastructure derivation
- architectural property observation

Failure, saturation, closure or non-reentry are also valid outcomes when structurally recorded.

---

## 19. CLOSURE STATES

Experiments may reach closure states.

Closure occurs when an experiment reaches a stable structural condition that does not require further intervention.

Possible closure conditions include:

- saturation of the experimental trajectory
- withdrawal without degradation of the system
- persistence of the system without continuous control
- non-reentry of the experimental regime

Closure is considered a valid experimental outcome.

---

## 20. DERIVATIVES

Derivatives may appear when accumulated trajectory reveals persistent structural patterns or operational regimes.

Examples include:

- new experimental regimes
- derived infrastructures
- structural protocols
- architectural patterns
- new operational environments

Derivatives are emergent outcomes and are not required for experiment validity.

---

## 21. ARCHITECTURAL OBSERVATION

Architectural observations occur when repeated experiments reveal persistent structural behavior.

Examples may include:

- persistence under withdrawal
- structural closure without degradation
- coordination without communication
- topology emergence from minimal acts

Observations must be supported by trajectory evidence.

---

## 22. RELATION TO INFRASTRUCTURES

REGIME defines the protocol.

Infrastructures provide execution environments.

Examples:

REGIME

↓

ENDO

↓

ENDO NODE

REGIME is infrastructure-agnostic.

ENDO is one possible infrastructure capable of satisfying REGIME conditions.

---

## 23. RELATION TO ARQUITECTURA SIMBIÓTICA

Arquitectura Simbiótica defines the structural architecture.

REGIME defines the experimental protocol under which that architecture may be explored through execution.

Arquitectura Simbiótica is the architectural domain.
REGIME is the operational experimental frame.
