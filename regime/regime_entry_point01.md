# REGIME — ENTRY POINT 01

## PURPOSE

This document defines the minimal entry condition required for an actor to execute a structural experiment within the REGIME protocol.

The entry point does not require prior membership, institutional affiliation or centralized authorization.

Execution within REGIME is defined by structural acts and persistent records.

Experiments occur within an execution regime or experimental environment.


---

## MINIMAL ENTRY CONDITION

An actor may enter REGIME when the following sequence occurs:

condition  
→ act  
→ record  
→ evidence  
→ trajectory


If the sequence exists, the experiment exists.


---

## ENTRY SEQUENCE

### 1. DECLARE CONDITION

A structural condition must be declared.

The condition defines the context in which the experiment occurs.

Examples:

Finite operational window  
Runtime activation  
Artifact release  
Node deployment  
Experimental regime declaration


---

### 2. EXECUTE ACT

A structural act must occur.

Examples:

emit structural cell  
release artifact  
deploy runtime  
execute infrastructure  
publish execution trace


---

### 3. PRODUCE RECORD

The act must generate a persistent record.

Examples:

artifact repository  
runtime output  
structural cell  
execution trace  
persistent system record


---

### 4. REGISTER EVIDENCE

Evidence must exist showing the act occurred.

Examples:

repository commit  
runtime log  
artifact reference  
persistent record identifier


---

### 5. INTEGRATE INTO TRAJECTORY

The experiment becomes part of the accumulating structural history of the system.


---

## EXECUTION TRACE IDENTIFICATION

Execution traces should include an identifier to allow reference and traceability.

Recommended format:

origin-ET##

Examples:

gmc-ET01  
nodeA-ET02  
labX-ET03  

Actors execute experiments.

Origins identify the emission source associated with records.

The origin identifier represents the emitting source and does not require global uniqueness.

Execution traces become uniquely identifiable through their persistent record and location.


---

## TRACE REFERENCES

Execution traces may reference previously recorded experiments or structural records.

References may include:

execution traces  
structural artifacts  
runtime records  
other persistent experiment records  

References may optionally include a location where the referenced record can be observed.

Reference identifiers typically follow the trace identifier format.

Examples:

gmc-ET01  
labA-ET04  


---

## VALID ENTRY

An actor has entered REGIME when the following exist:

structural condition  
+  
structural act  
+  
persistent record  
+  
observable evidence


No registration or permission is required.


---

## ACTORS

Actors may include:

human  
computational agents  
hybrid systems  
distributed infrastructures  

The protocol evaluates acts and records rather than actor identity.


---

## ENVIRONMENTS

REGIME experiments may occur in any environment capable of producing irreversible records.

Examples include:

experimental runtimes  
artifact repositories  
distributed infrastructures  
hybrid human-computational environments  


---

## RESULTS

Experiments executed through REGIME may produce:

structural artifacts  
execution traces  
runtime behavior  
topology changes  
regime transitions  
derived infrastructures  
architectural properties  


Closure, saturation, withdrawal or non-reentry are also valid results when recorded.


---

## DERIVATIVES

Persistent patterns observed within accumulated trajectory may generate derivatives.

Derivatives may include:

new regimes  
derived infrastructures  
structural protocols  
operational environments  
architectural patterns  


Derivatives emerge from trajectory and are not required for experiment validity.


---

## RULE

no act → no experiment  
no record → no evidence  
no trajectory → no system
