# Trajectory

The exploration of Arquitectura Simbiótica emerged through a sequence of structural investigations combining architecture, human activity and computational systems.

Early work focused on architectural thinking around environments capable of interacting with human processes and computational systems.

As the exploration progressed, the focus shifted toward identifying structural conditions under which hybrid systems may operate without relying entirely on centralized control structures.

This led to the formulation of minimal structural acts as the basic units capable of producing irreversible system history.

From these conditions, several experimental infrastructures and artifacts have been developed.

These include runtime environments designed to record structural acts and artifacts that document executed structural conditions.

The system continues to evolve through experimentation, structural observation and the release of selected artifacts.
