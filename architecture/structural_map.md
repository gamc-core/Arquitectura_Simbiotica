# STRUCTURAL MAP

## Premise

Arquitectura Simbiótica defines the structural conditions under which hybrid systems may operate and evolve.

The architecture does not prescribe outcomes.

Structural acts accumulate over time, forming trajectories that may reveal stable regimes, structural properties or derived infrastructures.

These trajectories are not predefined but emerge through execution.

---

## Structural Flow

```
REGIME  
↓  
ENDO  
↓  
ENDO NODE  
↓  
STRUCTURAL ACTS  
↓  
ARTIFACTS  
↓  
EXECUTION TRACES  
↓  
PUBLIC FEED  
↓  
STRUCTURAL TRAJECTORY
```

---

## Layers

The architecture is organized through the following structural layers.

---

### REGIME

Type: Protocol

Defines the operational conditions under which structural acts may occur.

- execution boundaries  
- temporal constraints  
- closure conditions  

REGIME does not execute.  
REGIME constrains.

---

### ENDO

Type: Generational infrastructure

Defines how structural acts are recorded, persisted and accumulated.

- generational partitioning  
- irreversible recording  
- internal integrity (hash chaining)  

ENDO does not interpret.  
ENDO persists.

---

### ENDO NODE

Type: Runtime implementation

Executes structural acts within a given REGIME and ENDO context.

- produces structural cells  
- advances generational state  
- operates without predefined objectives  

ENDO NODE executes.

---

### STRUCTURAL ACTS

Type: Events

Discrete acts produced during execution that may result in valid structural events.

- emitted by origins  
- sequenced within generations  
- validated through structural mutation  
- stored in ENDO as irreversible records  

Acts do not explain.  
Acts occur.

---

### ARTIFACTS

Type: External outputs

Concrete outputs produced through structural execution.

- CPR (structural conditions)  
- CE (structural executions)  

Artifacts extend the system externally.

---

### EXECUTION TRACES

Type: Recorded observations

Structured records describing observable execution events produced under REGIME.

- condition  
- act  
- record  
- trajectory  
- state  

Execution traces do not simulate.  
They document.

---

### PUBLIC FEED

Type: Observable layer derived from ENDO.

Exposes observable structural state externally.

- snapshots (state)  
- closures (cycle completion)  
- latest (current state pointer)  

Public feed enables observation without internal access.

---

## Invariants

- Structural acts are irreversible once recorded.  
- Generational boundaries define discrete execution cycles.  
- Public records reflect system state but do not control it.  
- Execution is not goal-directed.  
- Observation does not modify execution.  

---
